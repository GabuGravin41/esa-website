ESA Constitution

The official governing document of the Engineering Students Association at Kenyatta University

PREAMBLE

We, the students of the School of Engineering and Technology at Kenyatta University, in order to establish an effective means of communication and cooperation among ourselves, promote academic excellence, foster leadership skills, and enhance our professional development, do hereby establish this Constitution for the Engineering Students Association (ESA).

ARTICLE I: NAME AND IDENTITY

The name of this organization shall be the Engineering Students Association, hereinafter referred to as ESA. ESA shall be recognized as the official representative body of all students enrolled in engineering programs at Kenyatta University.

ARTICLE II: OBJECTIVES

The objectives of ESA shall be to:

  1. Represent the interests of engineering students to the university administration and relevant external bodies.
  2. Promote academic excellence within the engineering disciplines.
  3. Organize professional development activities including workshops, seminars, and industry visits.
  4. Foster collaboration and knowledge sharing among engineering students.
  5. Establish and maintain links with professional engineering bodies and the industry.
  6. Support student innovations and research projects.
  7. Provide a platform for social interaction among engineering students.

ARTICLE III: MEMBERSHIP

Section 1: Regular Membership
All students duly registered in any engineering program at Kenyatta University shall automatically be members of ESA.

Section 2: Rights of Members
All members shall have the right to:

  1. Participate in all activities organized by ESA.
  2. Vote in ESA elections and referendums.
  3. Contest for positions in ESA, subject to eligibility requirements.
  4. Access ESA resources and services.
  5. Attend and contribute to general meetings.

Section 3: Obligations of Members
All members shall:

  1. Abide by this Constitution and any regulations made pursuant to it.
  2. Pay membership fees as may be determined by the Executive Committee.
  3. Participate actively in ESA activities.
  4. Uphold the dignity and reputation of ESA.

ARTICLE IV: GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

The governance of ESA shall be vested in:

  1. The General Assembly
  2. The Executive Committee
  3. Standing Committees

ARTICLE V: THE EXECUTIVE COMMITTEE

Section 1: Composition
The Executive Committee shall consist of:

  1. Chairperson
  2. Vice-Chairperson
  3. Secretary General
  4. Treasurer
  5. Academic Secretary
  6. Social and Events Coordinator
  7. Public Relations Officer
  8. Class Representatives from each engineering course and year

ARTICLE VI: MEETINGS

Section 1: General Assembly Meetings
The General Assembly shall meet at least twice per semester.

Section 2: Executive Committee Meetings
The Executive Committee shall meet at least once a month.

ARTICLE VII: FINANCES

Section 1: Sources of Funds
ESA's funds shall be derived from:

  1. Membership fees
  2. University allocations
  3. Fundraising activities
  4. Donations and grants
  5. Income from ESA activities and investments

ARTICLE VIII: AMENDMENTS

This Constitution may be amended by a two-thirds majority vote of the General Assembly, provided that the proposed amendment has been circulated to all members at least two weeks prior to the vote.

ARTICLE IX: DISSOLUTION

In the event of dissolution, all assets of ESA shall be transferred to the School of Engineering and Technology to be used for student activities.

ARTICLE X: RATIFICATION

This Constitution shall come into effect upon approval by a simple majority of engineering students in a referendum and subsequent recognition by the university administration.

This is a sample constitution document. The actual ESA Constitution would contain more detailed provisions and may differ from this template. Please contact the ESA Executive Committee for the complete and current constitution.
